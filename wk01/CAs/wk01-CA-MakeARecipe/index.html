Singapore Cereal Prawns

Invented in Singapore zi char restaurants, this seafood dish is rather special. Succulent king prawns get a nostalgic coating in and the result is rich, sweet, savoury and crunchy. The perfect share plate, add these cereal prawns to your next Asian dinner banquet and dive in.

Ingredients:

  • 500g king prawns, shells on, deveined
  • 1 egg white
  • 1/2 cup cornflour
  • 1/2 cup dried curry leaves
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup milk powder
  • 2 tbsp sugar
Steps:
  1. In a bowl, combine egg white and cornflour. Toss prawns in the mixture until well coated.
  2. Heat oil in a wok over medium-high heat. Deep-fry prawns in batches until golden and cooked through. Drain on paper towels and set aside.
  3. In a clean wok, melt butter over medium heat. Add curry leaves and saut until fragrant.
  4. Add condensed milk, milk powder, and sugar. Stir until the mixture thickens slightly.
  5. Add the fried prawns back into the wok. Toss to coat the prawns evenly with the cereal mixture.
  6. Serve immediately as a delicious appetizer or main dish.
